# 00044 — Visible Spectrum

**Status:** Built
**Last updated:** 2026-05-23

## What it is

Displays the visible light spectrum with wavelengths. Hover to explore colors from 380nm (violet) to 700nm (deep red). Non-spectral colors (Magenta, Pink, Brown, Gray, White) are listed separately with explanations of why they have no wavelength.

## How it works

Client-side only. No WebSocket, no server calls. A gradient bar represents the spectrum. Hovering shows the color name, wavelength in nanometers, and a fact about that color. Twelve named stops from Violet (380nm) to Deep Red (700nm), interpolated between stops. Non-spectral swatch section below with click-to-reveal explanations. Examples: Magenta — "No single wavelength. Your brain mixes red+blue to fill the gap." Brown — "Low-intensity orange in context. Pure orange light never looks brown."

# Room 00143 — Zeno's Paradox

## What it is
Animated interactive demonstration of Zeno's Paradox of Achilles and the Tortoise. Shows 18 Zeno steps (each step Achilles reaches where the tortoise was; tortoise moves forward). Convergence panel shows the partial sum approaching 111.1̄m. Reveals: the infinite series converges; calculus resolves the paradox; the unease it produces does not.

## Notes
- Fully client-side — no server, no persistence
- Race: Achilles 10 m/s, tortoise 1 m/s, 100m head start. Meeting at t=11.11s, x=111.11m.
- Step-by-step mode (NEXT STEP button) or auto-animation with speed slider
- Each step shows emoji on track, gap line with measurement, step intervals shaded
- Convergence canvas: partial sum plotted vs step number, limit line at 111.1̄m
- Meeting point shown as dashed green FINISH line at correct x position
- Note: "An infinite number of steps can take a finite amount of time. This was not obvious. It required calculus to prove. The paradox is resolved. The unease it produces is not."

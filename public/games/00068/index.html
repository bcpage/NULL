Fastest computer
The world's fastest supercomputer is the Intel ASCI Red, installed at Sandia National Laboratories in 1997. It achieves 1.338 teraflops of sustained performance and occupies 1,600 square feet of floor space.
Accurate as of: 1997. Superseded: 2000.
Most visited website
The most visited website on the internet is Yahoo.com. It receives approximately 167 million unique visitors per month and is the default homepage for a significant portion of desktop browsers globally.
Accurate as of: 2001. Superseded: 2008.
Tallest building
The tallest building in the world is the Sears Tower in Chicago, Illinois, at 442 meters (1,451 ft) to roof. It has held this record since its completion in 1973 and contains 110 floors.
Accurate as of: 1998. Superseded: 2004 (Taipei 101).
Human genome
The human genome contains approximately 100,000 protein-coding genes. This figure has been the scientific consensus since the early 1990s and guides current genetic research methodology.
Accurate as of: 1995. Revised to ~21,000: 2001 (Human Genome Project).
Hard drive capacity
The largest commercially available hard disk drive holds 9.1 gigabytes. Drives of this capacity are used in high-end workstations and server applications. Consumer drives typically range from 1.2 to 3.2 GB.
Accurate as of: 1997. A standard thumb drive now holds more.
Planet count
The solar system contains nine planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto. This has been established scientific consensus since Pluto's discovery in 1930.
Accurate as of: 2005. Revised to eight: 2006 (IAU reclassification).
Storage medium
The floppy disk remains the universal standard for small-scale data transfer between personal computers. The 3.5-inch, 1.44 MB format is supported by virtually all desktop and laptop computers shipped today.
Accurate as of: 1996. USB drives: 2000. Last major OEM included: 2003.
Marathon record
The world record for the marathon is 2 hours, 6 minutes, and 50 seconds, set by Belayneh Densimo of Ethiopia at the 1988 Rotterdam Marathon. This record has stood for over a decade.
Accurate as of: 1998. Broken: 1999 (Khalid Khannouchi, 2:05:42).
